What is Closed Circuit Television Systems (CCTV)?

A Closed-Circuit Television (CCTV) system is a security solution that uses cameras and video recorders to monitor, capture, and store footage of specific areas. Unlike public broadcast systems, CCTV transmits footage to a designated set of monitors for private viewing, ensuring controlled and secure access to surveillance data. Modern CCTV systems feature advanced capabilities like HD video resolution, infrared night vision, pan-tilt-zoom (PTZ) functions, and AI-powered analytics for facial recognition or motion tracking.

CCTV systems are versatile, with applications ranging from small-scale installations in homes to complex multi-camera setups in large facilities. These systems enhance security by deterring potential intruders, assisting in incident investigations, and providing real-time monitoring for businesses and homeowners. With advancements in technology, many CCTV systems now support remote access via smartphones or computers, ensuring constant visibility and peace of mind.
