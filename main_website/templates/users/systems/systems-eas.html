What is Electronic Article Surveillance Systems?

An Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) system is a security technology used to detect and prevent the theft of items from retail environments. The system typically consists of security tags or labels that are attached to merchandise, and an array of detection antennas placed at store entrances or exits. If a tagged item passes through the detection zone without being deactivated or removed, the system triggers an alarm to alert store staff.

EAS systems use various types of technologies, such as radio frequency (RF), acousto-magnetic (AM), or electromagnetic (EM) systems, to detect unauthorized movements of tagged products. These systems are customizable and can be integrated with other security solutions, such as CCTV or access control systems, to provide comprehensive theft prevention strategies. EAS systems are commonly used in retail stores, libraries, and warehouses to protect against shoplifting and inventory loss.
